5 Ways to Get Involved With the Lafayette YWCA

By: Ben Scholer

The Lafayette YWCA is a local chapter of the National YWCA. They offer various services and events to local women for either a very low, or no charge. After reading this article, you will know what they offer, and how to get involved with them!

1) Domestic Violence Programs

If this an emergency, please call or text 911! If you or someone you know is stuck in a relationship with domestic violence involved, the Lafayette YWCA has a number of resources to help! From their women’s shelter to counseling and therapy programs, you’ll be able to find the right fit, and the right resources to help out yourself or a friend in need.

2) Women's Cancer Programs

With cancer as prevalent as it is in today’s day and age, it is ever increasingly important to be checked for signs of it regularly. At the YWCA, you can be screened for breast and cervical cancer free of charge, with or without insurance! Just call the YWCA at (765) 742-4375 to speak with a specialist to see if you qualify for the program.

3) Y-Dance Classes

Stressed about work or home life? Need a break? Or just need some fun physical activity? The Lafayette YWCA’s Y-Dance classes might be perfect for you. For an annual fee of only $25 (an average American coffee drinker spends more than $1,500 on coffee a year!), you can have access to a wide range of dance classes, from the basics up to more advanced forms of dance. See the YWCA’s website for more details.

4) Volleyball

Looking for a way to get active while also honing in your volleyball skills? Try out the YWCA’s volleyball program! They have teams for everyone, from children to adults! Just go to their website to register!

5) Pickleball

Are you looking to play a fun sport you’ve likely never tried? Try out Pickleball at the YWCA! Pickleball is often described as a cross between tennis, table tennis and badminton, and is a fun activity for all ages and skill levels! The fee to participate is $3 per day, $25 for a monthly pass, or $40 for a family pass! For more information about it, please see the details on the Lafayette YWCA website.

In conclusion, the Lafayette YWCA is a great place for women to go to, for everything from sports to cancer testing, with many free services. Hopefully, this article has informed you about some of the services and activities they offer!
